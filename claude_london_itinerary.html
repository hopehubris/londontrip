Monday, May 5 · Central London
Arrival + Covent Garden
Easy intro day — everything within 10 minutes of your hotel.
Afternoon
Check in — Travelodge Covent Garden
10 Drury Lane WC2B 5RE. Your home base for 15 nights. Drop bags, freshen up.
3:00 PM
Pandora at Covent Garden
~45 min · 3 min walk from hotel
21 James St WC2E. Open until 8pm Mon–Sat. Perfect first London errand.
4:00 PM
Leicester Square
~1 hour · 10 min walk
Street performers, buskers, the buzz of London's entertainment district. Easy stroll to get your bearings.
Evening
Covent Garden Market + Dinner
Steps from your hotel. Street performers, restaurants on every corner. Great first night introduction to London life.
Tips
  • No Tube needed today — everything is walkable from the hotel.
  • Get an Oyster card or set up contactless payment for the Tube — you'll need it from Day 4 onwards.

Tuesday, May 6 · Central London
National Gallery + Trafalgar Square + Forbidden Planet
All within a 10-minute cluster. All free. All brilliant.
10:00 AM
National Gallery
3–4 hours · 15 min walk from hotel
Trafalgar Square WC2N. Free entry. Van Gogh's Sunflowers, da Vinci, Rembrandt, Monet. One of the world's great collections.
Free entry
2:30 PM
Trafalgar Square
45 min · Right outside the Gallery
Nelson's Column, bronze lions, fountains. A classic London square that always rewards a wander.
Free
3:30 PM
Forbidden Planet London Megastore
1–2 hours · 179 Shaftesbury Ave WC2H
Sci-fi, comics, manga, anime, collectibles over two floors. Open until 7pm. Budget alert — the wallet rarely survives intact.
Tips
  • The National Gallery stays open until 9pm on Fridays if you want a quieter visit on another day.
  • Forbidden Planet is a 5-minute walk north from Trafalgar, up through the back of Leicester Square.
  • Eat lunch inside the Gallery café (downstairs) or grab something from one of the stalls around Trafalgar.

Wednesday, May 7 · Bloomsbury → Soho
British Museum + House of MinaLima
A morning of ancient wonders into an afternoon of wizarding design.
10:00 AM
The British Museum
3–4 hours · Great Russell St WC1B
Free entry. Rosetta Stone, Elgin Marbles, Egyptian mummies, Lewis Chessmen. One of the world's greatest museums. The Great Court alone is worth the visit.
Free entry
2:30 PM
House of MinaLima
1–2 hours · 157 Wardour St W1F (Soho)
The graphic design duo behind the Harry Potter films. Four floors of original artwork, prints, and merchandise. Gallery is free to enter. Open 11am–6:45pm daily.
Gallery is free
Tips
  • British Museum is free but book a timed entry slot online to avoid queuing outside.
  • Focus on 2–3 galleries rather than trying to see everything — Egyptian, Greek, and Medieval Europe are highlights.
  • MinaLima is 20 min walk from the museum through Soho's streets. Grab lunch en route.
  • MinaLima can get crowded — weekday afternoons are quieter.

Thursday, May 8 · East London (full day + evening)
The Who Shop + Jack the Ripper Museum + Evening Walk
A long but thrilling East London day — from Doctor Who to Victorian murder.
9:30 AM
The Who Shop
2–3 hours · 39–41 Barking Rd, East Ham E6
District line from Covent Garden/Aldgate East toward Barking — Upton Park station (~45 min). Doctor Who shop, museum, costumes, and memorabilia. Also stocks Buffy, sci-fi, and fantasy. Staff are legendary fans.
Thu–Sat only
1:30 PM
Jack the Ripper Museum
1–1.5 hours · 12 Cable St, Aldgate E1
Return west on the District/Circle line to Aldgate East (~35 min). Immersive recreation of Victorian East End. Five floors of social history — not just gory, genuinely thoughtful. Add £5 audio guide.
Book ahead for audio guide
~7:00 PM
Jack the Ripper Walking Tour
~2 hours · Departs from Aldgate East area
Evening walking tours depart from Aldgate East tube area. Book a tour separately — "Elegant Sam" from the museum's affiliated tours is highly reviewed. Walks the original murder sites and Victorian streets.
Evening — book in advance
Tips
  • Confirm The Who Shop's exact address at thewhoshop.com — they have moved premises in the past.
  • Eat lunch near East Ham before heading back — the Boleyn Pub on Green Street is a favourite local spot.
  • Leave comfortable shoes for the evening walk — 2 hours of cobbled Victorian streets.
  • The evening walking tour and museum are in the same area, so no extra travel needed between them.

Friday, May 9 · East London / Thames
Tower of London + Tower Bridge
Two icons, five minutes apart. Arrive early to beat the crowds.
9:00 AM
Tower of London
3–4 hours · Circle/District line to Tower Hill
Crown Jewels, Beefeater-guided tour, White Tower armory, the ravens. One of England's most storied sites. Book tickets online to skip the queue.
Pre-book tickets — hrp.org.uk
1:30 PM
Tower Bridge Exhibition
1–2 hours · 5 min walk from Tower
Walk the glass-floor walkways high above the Thames, see the Victorian engine rooms, and get unbeatable views up and down the river. The glass floor is genuinely exhilarating.
Book tickets at towerbridge.org.uk
3:30 PM
Borough Market + South Bank stroll
1–2 hours · 10 min walk across London Bridge
Cross London Bridge to Borough Market (open until 5pm) for some of London's best food stalls. Optional: continue west along the South Bank toward the Tate Modern.
Tips
  • Tower opens at 9am Tue–Sat, 10am Sunday. Arriving at opening beats the midday crush significantly.
  • Book the Beefeater guided tour — free with admission, runs at regular intervals, essential for context.
  • The Crown Jewels queue can be long — go there first thing before it builds.
  • Tower Bridge bookings recommended especially in spring/summer.

Saturday, May 10 · South Kensington
Natural History Museum + Science Museum
Next-door neighbours. Both free. Both extraordinary. Full day required.
10:00 AM
Natural History Museum
3 hours · Cromwell Rd SW7
Free entry. Hope the blue whale skeleton in Hintze Hall, dinosaur gallery, mammals gallery, and the stunning Romanesque architecture. Pre-book free timed entry online.
Free entryReserve timed entry online
2:00 PM
Science Museum
3 hours · Exhibition Rd SW7 (right next door)
Free entry. Space gallery, flight gallery with full-size aircraft, the Apollo 10 command module, and interactive science exhibits. Equally as impressive as the NHM.
Free entry
Tips
  • Both museums are on the same street (Exhibition Road) — you'll be walking 30 seconds between them.
  • Pick your priority galleries in each rather than attempting everything — you'd need two full days for both.
  • NHM: prioritise Hintze Hall (whale), Dinosaurs, and the wildlife garden if the weather's nice.
  • South Kensington station is right there; take the Piccadilly line from Holborn (easy from Covent Garden area).

Sunday, May 11 · Kensington + Earl's Court
Kensington Gardens + Earl's Court + Freddie Mercury
A gentle Sunday — parks, statues, bohemian streets, and a rock legend's home.
10:30 AM
Diana Princess of Wales Memorial Fountain
45 min · Hyde Park W2
A beautiful oval stream of Cornish granite running through Hyde Park. Peaceful and moving. Children love paddling in the shallows in warm weather.
11:30 AM
Peter Pan Statue
30 min · Kensington Gardens W2
10 min walk through the gardens. The 1912 bronze by George Frampton, commissioned overnight by J.M. Barrie as a surprise for London. Surrounded by squirrels and fairy folk (allegedly).
1:30 PM
Freddie Mercury's Former Home
30 min · 1 Logan Place W8, Earl's Court
The Garden Lodge — covered in fan tributes, flowers, and messages. No interior access, but the wall itself is a deeply moving experience for Queen fans. 10 min Tube from Kensington.
2:30 PM
Earl's Court Neighbourhood
1–2 hours
Explore Earl's Court Road — vintage shops, independent restaurants, and the Warwick Road area. A historic bohemian London neighbourhood worth a leisurely afternoon wander.
Tips
  • Both Hyde Park and Kensington Gardens are open 24/7 and free.
  • The Diana Fountain can close in bad weather — check royalparks.org.uk before visiting.
  • From Peter Pan statue to Earl's Court: take the 49 bus down Kensington High Street, or Tube from High St Kensington (one stop to Earl's Court).
  • Logan Place is a quiet residential street — be respectful of neighbours.

Monday, May 12 · South Bank
London Eye + South Bank Waterfront
Best views in London, followed by one of its finest walks.
10:00 AM
London Eye
1.5–2 hours · South Bank SE1
Pre-book tickets online — sells out fast. Opens 11am Mon–Thu, 10am Fri–Sun. The 30-min rotation gives 360° views up to 25 miles on a clear day. Consider the Champagne Experience for a special treat.
Must pre-book — londoneye.com
12:30 PM
South Bank Waterfront Walk
2–3 hours · Walk east along the Thames
From the Eye: walk east past Hungerford Bridge, National Theatre, BFI, Tate Modern (free to enter), Millennium Bridge, and Borough Market. One of London's great free experiences.
Entirely free
Tips
  • The Eye opens at 11am on Mondays — book the first slot to avoid building queues.
  • The South Bank walk can extend all the way to Tower Bridge (~3 miles total), linking back to Day 5 territory.
  • Tate Modern is free and worth 30–45 minutes inside even if you're not an art fan — the Turbine Hall is spectacular.
  • Borough Market is closed on Mondays and Sundays (open Tue–Sat). Check for current hours.

Doctor Who Walking Tour — Brit Movie Tours
Allons-y! A guided walk through London's real-life Whovian filming locations.
Morning
Doctor Who Walking Tour
~2–3 hours · Brit Movie Tours
A guided walk of London locations used in Doctor Who filming. Your booking confirmation will include the exact meeting point. Brit Movie Tours office is at 7 Bell Yard WC2A — close to your hotel. Book at britmovietours.com
Pre-book at britmovietours.com
Afternoon
Free afternoon
Use the afternoon to revisit favourite areas, do some shopping around Covent Garden, or explore the Strand and Aldwych area near the Brit Movie Tours office. Fleet Street and the Inns of Court are worth a wander.
Tips
  • Book well in advance — Doctor Who tours are popular and fill quickly, especially in spring.
  • Check britmovietours.com for current tour schedules and meeting point details.
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes — tours cover significant ground over 2+ hours.
  • Reviews highlight guide "Chris" as particularly fun and knowledgeable on Doctor Who tours.

Bridgerton Walking Tour — Brit Movie Tours
The ton awaits. A tour through Mayfair and London's most elegant filming locations.
Morning
Bridgerton Walking Tour
~2–3 hours · Brit Movie Tours
Filming locations include streets of Mayfair, South Kensington, and other elegant London districts. Check britmovietours.com for the Bridgerton tour schedule and exact meeting point — it varies by season.

Thursday, May 15 · West London
Sherlock's Mind Palace Afternoon Tea
Elementary, my dear traveller. A speakeasy tea with a mystery to solve.
12:00–3:45 PM
Sherlock's Mind Palace Afternoon Tea
~90 min · W12 Centre, Shepherd's Bush
Enter through the front of "Doyle's Opticians" at W12 Shopping Centre. Inside: a secret Sherlock-themed speakeasy with sandwiches, scones, cakes, tea, and a mini mystery to solve at your table. Available slots: 12:15, 1:00, 1:45, 2:45, or 3:45pm.
Thu–Sun onlyBook at sherlockescaperooms.com
Afternoon
Shepherd's Bush / Westfield
After tea, Westfield Shepherd's Bush is one of London's largest shopping centres right next door. Or take the Central line one stop to Holland Park for a beautiful garden walk.
Tips
  • Nearest Tube: Shepherd's Bush (Central line, ~20 min from Holborn).
  • Minimum 2 people required; email dietary requirements 72 hours in advance.
  • The boozy version (bottomless Prosecco 90 mins) is available for 18+ groups — highly recommended!
  • The "Doyle's Opticians" entrance is part of the experience — don't be confused by the shopfront.

Friday, May 16 · Day Trip to Kent
Leeds Castle
"The loveliest castle in the world" — surrounded by a moat, a maze, and 900 years of history.
8:30 AM
Depart from London Bridge Station
Train to Bearsted station (~55 min). Trains run approximately every 30 min. Then take the shuttle bus to the castle (timed to coincide with trains).
10:00 AM
Leeds Castle
Full day · Broomfield, Maidstone ME17
Audio tour of the castle rooms. The estate maze (trickier than it looks). Falconry shows. Beautiful parkland and lake. A kids' play area. Plenty to fill a whole day. The ticket is valid for return visits for a full year.
Book at leeds-castle.com
~4:00 PM
Return to London
Shuttle bus back to Bearsted, train to London Bridge. Back in central London by about 5:30pm.
Tips
  • Bring your own picnic — the castle café is lovely but pricey.
  • The castle closes at 4pm so plan your day accordingly.
  • Leeds Castle is NOT in Leeds (it's named after a person). It's in Kent, southeast of London.
  • Check the shuttle bus timetable when booking — it coordinates with specific trains from London Bridge.

Saturday, May 17 · Day Trip to Kent
Hever Castle
Anne Boleyn's childhood home — intimate, authentic, and utterly romantic.
8:30 AM
Depart from London Victoria Station
Train to Hever station (~1 hour). A rural single-track line through beautiful Kent countryside. Trains are infrequent — check timetables carefully for your return.
10:30 AM
Hever Castle & Gardens
Full day · Hever, Edenbridge TN8
A 15–20 min walk from the station through a field path (can be muddy — wear boots or sturdy shoes). The castle itself is small, intimate, and brilliantly preserved. The Italian gardens, topiary maze, and lake are outstanding. Open 10:30am–6pm.
Book at hevercastle.co.uk
Lunch
King Henry VIII Pub, Hever Village
The village pub next to the castle entrance — charming 16th-century pub, excellent food, and an atmosphere perfectly matching the setting. Reviewers rave about it.
Tips
  • Trains from Hever back to London are roughly every 2 hours — download the timetable before you go.
  • The field path from Hever station can be muddy in spring — bring waterproof shoes.
  • Hever feels more personal and less crowded than Leeds Castle — a wonderful contrast.
  • The military museum on the estate grounds is a hidden gem — free with castle admission.

Sunday, May 18 · Day Trip to Gloucestershire
Gloucester Cathedral + optional Cheltenham
Harry Potter's cloisters, one of England's finest cathedrals, and a beautiful Regency spa town.
7:30 AM
Depart from London Paddington
GWR train to Gloucester (~1hr 45min). Book in advance for best fares. Trains run regularly from Paddington.
9:30 AM
Gloucester Cathedral
2–3 hours · College Green GL1
Free entry (donation welcomed). The fan-vaulted cloisters used in Harry Potter films are breathtaking. Book a guided tour (£8 per person) for expert context on 1,300 years of history. The whispering gallery views from above are worth the climb.
Free entryGuided tour recommended
1:00 PM
Cheltenham (optional)
2 hours · 30 min by bus from Gloucester
A beautiful Regency spa town with elegant Georgian terraces, the famous Promenade, independent boutiques, and excellent restaurants. Stagecoach bus 94 runs every 10 min between Gloucester and Cheltenham.
Tips
  • Book Paddington train tickets in advance for much cheaper fares (GWR Advance tickets).
  • The cathedral is quieter on Sunday mornings — you may catch a choral service (free to attend).
  • If skipping Cheltenham, explore Gloucester Docks instead — beautiful Victorian warehouses turned restaurants.
  • Return train from Gloucester to Paddington has good frequency throughout the afternoon.

Monday, May 19 · Departure Day
Final Morning + Farewell London
Savour the last few hours. London will be here when you return.
Morning
Covent Garden Market
Steps from your hotel. Open from 10am. Last chance for London souvenirs, handmade crafts, and a proper goodbye coffee in the piazza.
Flexible
Any remaining sites
If there are any missed stops from the itinerary — the Strand, Somerset House, or just a final walk along the Thames — today is the day. Keep it light and enjoyable.
Airport transport reminders
  • Heathrow: Piccadilly line from Holborn (direct, ~50 min, from Covent Garden area) or Heathrow Express from Paddington (15 min, pricier).
  • Gatwick: Gatwick Express from Victoria (30 min) or Southern/Thameslink (45 min, cheaper).
  • St Pancras (Eurostar): Northern line from Covent Garden area (~15 min).
  • Allow 3 hours from hotel departure for international flights; 2 hours for domestic.
